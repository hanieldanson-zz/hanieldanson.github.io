Search First, Ask Questions Later

01/11/2015

Writing or forming a good question can be tough, especially when the question is technical. Fortunately, I have quite a few years of experience asking technical questions on forums. My hope is that this will translate into solid questions on developer websites like Stack Overflow.

Over the years, I have usually received very helpful responses to my questions on technical forums. The biggest part of that is that I have taken the time to read forum guidelines. Most forums have very clear rules or guidelines on how to post. If you are posting a question that has been asked recently or is easily searchable, chances are that someone is going to get frustrated. That's why I always make sure there aren't previous threads that contain the answer to my question.

It is fairly common for people to get called out on forums for asking questions that have already been answered. When I do post a technical question on a technical forum, I start by mentioning that I have searched extensively for an answer to no avail. Perhaps I didn't search for the right terms, but I want people to know that I tried to find an answer first. This seems to do the trick.

I also try to be as detailed as possible. Any logs, specs, or other technical information I can provide will help on multiple levels. People are way more likely to respond to my question if I am detailed and concise. It's also a time saving measure. If I can provide all of the relevant information needed to answer my question up front, my question usually gets answered much more quickly.

I try to take the same approach if I'm asking someone for help with development questions. I have a good friend who I usually ask questions when I am completely stumped on something. The key for me is to give him as much detail up front as possible. I want him to be able to dive in and not feel like he has to ask me a ton of questions to figure out what I'm talking about. This approach has worked very well so far.

Daniel E. Hanson
